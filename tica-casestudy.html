Capstone Project

// UI/UX Case Study

TICA

Speech Therapy Through Play

Designing an accessible, engaging mobile application that helps children with Apraxia practice speech exercises through interactive daily challenges and personalized learning paths.

Role UI/UX Designer & Developer
Timeline 6 Months
Tools Figma, Python, Kivy

Visual Identity

Design in Motion

The visual identity of TICA is built upon a foundation of high-contrast accessibility and empathetic engagement, utilizing a vibrant sunset orange palette to provide warmth and clarity for young users.

01 / 04

01 / Challenge & Context

The Problem

Understanding the needs of children with Apraxia and designing an experience that makes therapy enjoyable and consistent.

👥

Target Audience

Children aged 5–12 with childhood apraxia of speech (CAS) require consistent, engaging practice. Current speech therapy apps lack the motivational design necessary to maintain long-term engagement in daily exercises.

🎯

Design Constraints

The interface had to accommodate varying motor skills, attention spans, and learning paces. Every interaction needed to feel supportive, never punitive, with clear progress feedback to maintain motivation.

03 / Key Features

Solution Highlights

Design decisions driven by accessibility, engagement, and effectiveness.

🎮
Gamified Exercises
Transform speech drills into engaging mini-games with reward systems, achievement badges, and progress streaks to maintain motivation.
🎤
Voice Recognition
Provides instant feedback, celebrating successes while gently guiding users toward correct pronunciation.
📊
Progress Tracking
Comprehensive dashboard for parents and therapists showing improvement metrics, consistency patterns, and areas needing focus.
🎨
Inclusive Design
Adaptive typography, color contrast optimization, and customizable interface settings to support diverse learning needs.
⏰
Smart Scheduling
Personalized exercise routines based on therapeutic recommendations with flexible, bite-sized sessions perfect for young learners.
👨‍👩‍👧
Family Integration
Parents can participate in exercises, reinforcing learning at home and strengthening the therapeutic connection between child and caregiver.

04 / Design Principles

UI/UX Foundations

Core principles that guided every interface decision.

Principle 01

Celebrate Effort

Every attempt is acknowledged positively. The app rewards participation and progress, never punishment or failure, building confidence in young users.

Principle 02

Clarity Over Complexity

Clear visual hierarchy, simple navigation, and unambiguous instructions prevent cognitive overload while maintaining therapeutic engagement.

Principle 03

Adaptive Pace

The interface respects individual learning speeds. Difficulty scales intelligently, ensuring challenges remain motivating without becoming frustrating.

05 / Design Evolution

Before & After

A side-by-side comparison showing how TICA evolved from initial concepts to the final polished design, highlighting key improvements in user experience and functionality.

Initial Design

Final Product

Initial Concept

Visual Design

Basic wireframes with minimal visual hierarchy. Limited use of color and typography, focusing primarily on functionality over aesthetics.

User Flow

Linear navigation with basic screens. No gamification elements or adaptive pacing. Users had to manually navigate between exercises.

Accessibility

Standard font sizes and basic contrast ratios. No adaptive features for different motor skills or attention spans.

Engagement

Simple progress bars with no rewards or motivational elements. Exercises felt like traditional therapy worksheets.

Final Design

Visual Design

Polished interface with custom typography, vibrant color schemes, and playful illustrations. Clear visual hierarchy guides users through interactions.

User Flow

Intuitive navigation with gamified exercises and smart scheduling. Adaptive difficulty scaling and personalized learning paths enhance engagement.

Accessibility

Optimized for diverse needs with adjustable settings, high contrast options, and inclusive design principles. Supports various motor skills and learning paces.

Engagement

Rich reward systems with badges, streaks, and achievements. Voice recognition provides real-time feedback, making therapy feel like play.
